## Which course do I need? (career route)
- Never scaffolded before → start with CISRS Level 1 (no prerequisites; head for heights + steel-toe boots is all you need).
- Want to erect/alter/strike scaffolds professionally → the CISRS route: Level 1 → Level 2 → Level 3.
- Topping out: Level 2+ can branch to Scaffolder Supervisor (run the job) or Scaffold Inspection (sign scaffolds off).
- Work NEAR scaffolds / at height but don't build them (trades, supervisors, riggers) → Getmie Safe Working at Height.
- Teams needing emergency readiness → Getmie Safe Rescue (basic → advanced; refresher to stay current).

## New delegate FAQ
- Experience needed for Level 1? No — basic fitness, a head for heights and steel-toe boots. PPE and tools are provided.
- What to bring: photo ID and steel-toe boots. Helmets, harnesses, gloves, tools and materials are supplied. Wear work clothes.
- Format: roughly 70% hands-on in the training yard, plus theory sessions and a full written + practical assessment.
- Certificate validity: most CISRS OTS certifications are valid for three years — book your refresher before expiry.
- Failed the assessment? Detailed instructor feedback and one resit is included in the course fee.
- Group bookings: yes — dedicated sessions for crews; contact the office for group rates.
